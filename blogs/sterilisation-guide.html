Hijama Equipment Sterilisation: A Complete Safety Guide

Proper sterilisation is non-negotiable in hijama practice. Any lapse in infection control puts patients at serious risk. This guide covers evidence-based sterilisation protocols for every type of hijama equipment.

Single-Use Items — Never Reuse

Surgical blades, lancet tips, and latex gloves must be disposed of after a single patient use. No exceptions. Place used blades in a sharps container immediately after use. Double-bag used gloves for clinical waste disposal.

Cleaning Hijama Cups Between Patients

After each patient: (1) Rinse cups under running water to remove gross contamination. (2) Wash with surgical scrub or dish soap. (3) Rinse thoroughly. (4) Soak in 70% isopropyl alcohol for 10 minutes. (5) Air-dry on a clean surface. Do not towel-dry as this can re-contaminate.

Autoclave for Glass Cups

Borosilicate glass fire cups can be autoclaved at 134°C for 3 minutes (porous cycle) or 121°C for 15 minutes (gravity cycle). This achieves full sterilisation and is the gold standard for reusable glass equipment. PVC cups are not autoclave-compatible.

Chemical Disinfectants

70% isopropyl alcohol is effective against most pathogens encountered in hijama practice and is the recommended surface and equipment disinfectant. Alternatively, 0.5% sodium hypochlorite (dilute bleach) can be used for soaking PVC cups but may reduce their lifespan.

Treatment Room Hygiene

Wipe down the treatment couch with disinfectant between each patient. Use a fresh disposable sheet or sterilised towel. Keep your equipment tray covered when not in use. Wash hands with surgical scrub before and after every patient. Keep a sharps container within arm's reach at all times.
