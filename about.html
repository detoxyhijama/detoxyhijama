Our Manufacturing Story

How Detoxy Hijama Became India's Leading Hijama Manufacturer

The Problem We Set Out to Solve

When our founder Mohamed Aasiq began researching the hijama therapy market in India in 2018, he found a broken supply chain. Indian hijama practitioners were paying ₹25–₹50 per cup at retail — for products that were often manufactured for less than ₹5 in large factories, then marked up by importers, distributors, wholesalers and retailers before reaching the end user.

Quality was also inconsistent. Without transparency into manufacturing processes, practitioners never knew whether the cups they ordered would have the same wall thickness, suction characteristics and durability as the previous batch. Sub-standard cups crack, lose suction prematurely, or have rough edges that damage patient skin — all problems that damage practitioner reputation.

The answer was simple but required significant investment: build India's own dedicated hijama manufacturing facility. Source medical-grade materials directly. Control every step of production. And sell factory-direct to practitioners at prices that reflect the true cost of manufacturing, not the cost of a bloated supply chain.

Building the Factory in Pollachi

Pollachi, in the Coimbatore district of Tamil Nadu, was chosen as the factory location for several reasons: proximity to India's established plastics manufacturing ecosystem in Coimbatore, excellent logistics connectivity to all parts of India via road and rail, a skilled industrial workforce, and competitive land and operational costs relative to metro cities.

The factory was built from the ground up with medical device manufacturing standards in mind — clean-room assembly areas, automated quality control lines, strict raw material testing protocols, and temperature-controlled storage for consumables. Today the facility spans thousands of square feet, operates multiple injection moulding machines around the clock, and employs over 200 workers including engineers, quality technicians, warehouse staff and logistics coordinators.

Our annual production capacity runs into millions of cups — making us, by output volume, the largest dedicated hijama cup manufacturer on the Indian subcontinent. This scale is what makes factory pricing possible for every customer, regardless of order size.

Our Manufacturing Standards & Materials

Raw Material Sourcing

We source only food-grade, BPA-free polypropylene resin certified to FDA 21 CFR and EU REACH standards. Every raw material batch is tested for purity and chemical composition before entering our production floor. We reject materials that fail our specifications — even if it means delays in production.

Injection Moulding Precision

Our moulds are machined to tolerances of ±0.05mm for critical dimensions including rim diameter, wall thickness and valve seat geometry. Consistent wall thickness ensures uniform suction pressure characteristics across every cup in a batch — critical for practitioners who rely on predictable suction levels in their clinical protocol.

Quality Inspection Protocol

Every cup leaving our production line passes through a multi-point inspection: visual inspection for surface defects and clarity (for premium transparent cups), dimensional check for rim diameter and depth, suction test at standard pressure, and random batch testing for material properties. Our rejection rate is less than 0.5% — one of the lowest in the industry.

Our Manufacturing Story

Built from the Ground Up — India's Dedicated Hijama Factory

Why We Built a Dedicated Hijama Factory

Most manufacturing businesses start with a product and find a market. Detoxy Hijama started differently — we started with a problem and built the solution from scratch. The problem: Indian hijama practitioners were overpaying for imported or re-labelled products of inconsistent quality, because no Indian manufacturer was taking the hijama market seriously enough to build dedicated production capacity for it.

The solution required real commitment — not a trading company sourcing products from generic factories and slapping a label on them, but a genuine manufacturing operation with purpose-built tooling, dedicated production lines, raw material quality control, and deep technical knowledge of how hijama products are actually used in clinical practice. This is what we built in Pollachi, Coimbatore.

Our founder spent two years researching the global hijama product supply chain before opening the factory — visiting manufacturers in China, studying material science, consulting with senior hijama practitioners about their product requirements, and building the technical team needed to make it work. The result is a factory that understands hijama not just as a product category, but as a clinical practice with specific technical and quality requirements.

Today, five years after opening, our factory produces millions of cups annually, supplies over 5,000 active B2B customers, and has established Detoxy Hijama as the trusted supply partner for some of India's largest hijama clinic networks. None of this happened by accident — it is the result of a clear mission, relentless quality focus, and genuine care for the practitioners and patients our products serve.

Our Pollachi Factory: Specifications and Capacity

Pollachi is located in the Coimbatore district of Tamil Nadu, 40 km from Coimbatore city — India's largest plastics manufacturing hub outside Mumbai. This proximity gives us immediate access to a mature ecosystem of raw material suppliers, tooling manufacturers, packaging specialists and logistics providers — essential infrastructure for efficient, high-quality production.

The factory occupies a purpose-built facility in an industrial estate outside Pollachi town. Production areas include the main injection moulding hall (housing multiple machines), a QC inspection and testing laboratory, a clean-room assembly area for kits and multi-component products, temperature-controlled storage for consumables, a finished goods warehouse, and packing and dispatch areas. Total built area exceeds several thousand square metres.

Our injection moulding machines operate on a 6-day production week with multiple shifts, giving us the capacity to produce millions of cups per year. Custom orders and new product development are handled on dedicated machines separate from the main production run, ensuring existing customers' supply is never disrupted by new tooling commissioning.

The factory employs over 200 people directly — injection moulding operators, QC technicians, assembly workers, warehouse staff, logistics coordinators and administrative staff. We take seriously our role as an employer in the Pollachi community and are committed to fair wages, safe working conditions and employee development.

Raw Materials

Medical-grade BPA-free polypropylene sourced from certified suppliers. All incoming material batches tested for melt flow index, density and chemical purity. We reject materials that fail spec — zero compromise.

Tooling & Moulds

All our moulds are precision machined to ±0.05mm tolerance on critical dimensions. Dedicated moulds for every cup size ensure dimensional consistency across millions of units. Moulds are maintained and refurbished on a scheduled preventive maintenance programme.

QC & Inspection

100% rim and surface inspection on every cup. Statistical sampling for dimensional conformance. Suction retention tests on every production batch. Rejection rate below 0.5%. QC records maintained for every production batch for full traceability.

Our Values

The Principles Behind Every Product We Make

Quality Without Compromise

We manufacture products that practitioners stake their reputation on and patients trust with their health. This is not an abstraction — it is the most concrete responsibility in our business. A cup that cracks mid-session, a glove that is not properly sealed, a blade that is not sharp enough for clean incisions — these failures have real consequences for real people.

Our quality commitment begins with raw material specification and ends with dispatch. Every step in between — moulding, inspection, assembly, packaging — has defined quality gates that product must pass before moving to the next stage. We invest heavily in QC because we believe quality is the foundation of the trust that makes our business possible.

We are also honest about the limits of any product. We do not make marketing claims that our products cannot support. We describe our products accurately so practitioners can make properly informed purchasing decisions. When a product is not suitable for a specific application, we say so.

Accessible Factory Pricing

We believe that the healing benefits of hijama should not be limited by the cost of equipment. When practitioners can access professional-grade cups at ₹8–₹12 each instead of ₹25–₹50, two things happen: established practitioners can operate more economically and serve more patients, and new practitioners can start their practice without the barrier of expensive equipment investment.

Factory pricing is not a promotional offer or a temporary discount — it is our permanent, sustainable business model. We eliminated every layer of the traditional supply chain — importer, national distributor, regional distributor, wholesaler, retailer — and sell direct from factory to practitioner. The cost savings from this model flow entirely to our customers in the form of lower prices, not to intermediary profits.

Our bulk pricing thresholds are set at realistic order quantities for active practices — not the enormous minimum orders that force practitioners to over-invest in inventory. A clinic doing 20 sessions per week can reach our best bulk pricing tier without tying up excessive capital in stock.
