Are the Premium Silicone Cups safe for wet hijama?

Yes. The Detoxy Hijama Premium Silicone Cups are made from 100% medical-grade BPA-free silicone that is autoclave-safe and boil-safe. They can be fully sterilised between sessions, making them suitable for both wet and dry hijama in professional clinic settings and home use.

How do I create suction with silicone cups?

Silicone cups require no pump. Simply compress the cup body with your fingers, place the open end on the skin, and release — the cup springs back and creates suction automatically. This makes silicone cups the easiest and safest option for beginners and self-cupping at home.

How do I clean and sterilise silicone hijama cups?

Clean silicone cups after each use with warm water and antiseptic soap. For sterilisation, boil in water for 10 minutes or use an autoclave. Alternatively, wipe with 70% isopropyl alcohol and allow to air dry. Store in a clean, sealed container away from direct sunlight.

How long do silicone hijama cups last?

With proper care, Detoxy Hijama Premium Silicone Cups last 2–5 years of regular use. The medical-grade silicone maintains its elasticity and suction ability through hundreds of uses. Replace cups if you notice cracking, permanent deformation, or loss of suction ability.
