Wet Cupping Technique: Step-by-Step Clinical Guide

Wet cupping (al-hijama) is a traditional therapeutic procedure that combines vacuum suction with controlled superficial incisions to draw out stagnant blood. This guide outlines a safe, hygienic clinical protocol suitable for trained practitioners.

Equipment You'll Need

Before starting, prepare: medical-grade hijama cups (sizes 2–4 for most body areas), a suction gun or silicone squeeze cups, sterile surgical blades (No.11), latex examination gloves, surgical cotton, 70% isopropyl alcohol for skin preparation, and sterile gauze.

Patient Preparation

Confirm the patient has not eaten a heavy meal in the past 2 hours. Have them lie comfortably in a prone position for back points. Clean the treatment area with alcohol swabs and allow to dry for 30 seconds. Mark the points if needed.

Dry Cupping Phase

Apply cups to the marked points using suction gun or squeeze method. Leave cups in place for 3–5 minutes. This draws blood to the surface and softens the skin. Remove cups gently by releasing the valve, not by pulling.

Incision Phase

Using a fresh sterile No.11 surgical blade for each point, make a series of very shallow parallel micro-incisions (1–2mm depth) across the cupped area. Do not cut deeply — the goal is to breach the superficial skin layer only.

Second Cupping Phase

Replace cups immediately over the incision sites. Blood will draw into the cup within 1–3 minutes. Leave cups for 5–7 minutes. Dispose of blood safely as clinical waste. Remove cups and clean each site with cotton and antiseptic.

Aftercare Instructions

Advise patients to keep treated areas dry for 24 hours. Avoid hot showers, swimming, or heavy exertion on the day of treatment. Apply antiseptic cream if any irritation occurs. Schedule a follow-up session no sooner than 3–4 weeks later.
