Silicone Moving Cup — Dynamic Therapy for Fascia and Muscle

The Detoxy Hijama Silicone Moving Cup is the practitioner's tool for dynamic gliding cupping therapy — a technique that produces myofascial release through longitudinal stretching of the fascia and soft tissue beneath the moving cup. Unlike stationary cupping, gliding cupping works in motion, continuously engaging new tissue fibre orientations and producing a deep, broad fascial release that static cupping cannot replicate.

Moving cup therapy is widely adopted in sports therapy settings, where practitioners use it for post-workout recovery, IT band syndrome treatment, thoracic mobility work, and pre-competition muscle priming. The technique requires an oil medium and a flexible silicone cup that can maintain suction during gliding motion without losing seal — properties that the Detoxy Moving Cup is specifically engineered to deliver.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is moving cup therapy (gliding cupping)?

Moving cup therapy applies suction with a cup slid across oiled skin, rather than held stationary. This creates myofascial release — stretching fascia and soft tissue longitudinally. Used for sports massage, IT band syndrome, muscle tightness, and improving range of motion.

How much oil is needed for moving cup therapy?

Apply a generous layer of massage oil, body oil, or coconut oil to the target area. The cup should glide smoothly without friction. Reapply oil every 2-3 minutes during a session. Too little oil causes drag and discomfort; too much prevents adequate suction.

Which conditions respond best to moving cup therapy?

IT band syndrome, thoracic and lumbar fascial tightness, post-workout muscle recovery, cellulite reduction, lymphatic oedema in limbs, and general back stiffness. Increasingly popular in sports therapy.

Can moving cups be used in wet hijama?

Moving cups are for dry cupping only. For wet hijama (with incisions), stationary cups are required as the cup must remain fixed over the incision site. Moving cupping before the incision phase as a warm-up is incorporated in some advanced protocols.

How do I clean the silicone moving cup?

Wash with warm water and mild soap after each use. For clinical settings, soak in medical disinfectant for 20 minutes, rinse, and air dry. Autoclave-safe for maximum sterilisation between patients.
