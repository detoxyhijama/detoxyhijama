Auto Lancet Pen — Precision Incisions for Wet Hijama

The Detoxy Hijama Auto Lancet Pen represents a significant advancement in wet hijama technique — replacing the manual blade method with a spring-loaded precision mechanism that delivers consistent, controlled incisions at the practitioner's chosen depth. This consistency translates directly into improved patient experience (less discomfort from irregular incisions) and better clinical outcomes (predictable bleed volume and distribution).

Why Consistent Incision Depth Matters

In wet hijama, the incision depth determines the therapeutic response. Too shallow and the capillary network in the superficial dermis is not adequately accessed — resulting in insufficient blood extraction. Too deep and the deeper dermal vessels are cut, causing excessive bleeding and increased healing time. The optimal incision depth of 1.2-1.6mm reaches the subepidermal capillary plexus precisely — the target for wet hijama therapy according to contemporary clinical protocols.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the auto lancet pen work?

Load a sterile lancet head, set the desired depth on the depth dial, cock the spring, press the tip to skin. The spring fires the lancet to the set depth and immediately retracts — creating a precise, consistent incision with minimal pain through the spring-loaded mechanism.

What depth setting should I use for hijama?

Standard back session: Depth 2-3 (1.2-1.4mm). Sensitive areas like the neck: Depth 1 (0.8mm). Thick skin or large patients: Depth 4-5 (1.6-1.8mm). Always start at a lower depth and adjust based on bleed response.

Are lancet heads single-use?

Yes — each lancet head must be discarded after single-patient use. Never reuse lancets as the tip deforms after first incision, creating irregular cuts and serious infection cross-contamination risk.

How many incisions per cup position?

Standard protocol: 3-5 incisions per cup position in cross or circular pattern, 3-5mm apart. Follow your hijama training protocols precisely regarding incision count and pattern.

Is this pen compatible with standard pharmacy lancets?

This pen uses standard 28G lancets widely available at pharmacies. In an emergency, standard diabetic lancets from major brands are compatible, though Detoxy lancets are calibrated specifically for hijama incision depths.
