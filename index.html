Frequently Asked Questions — Hijama Cupping Therapy & Equipment

What is hijama and how does it work?

Hijama (Al-Hijamah) is the ancient practice of therapeutic cupping — placing suction cups on the skin to create negative pressure that draws blood and toxins to the surface, decompresses muscles and fascia, stimulates blood flow, and activates the body's natural healing mechanisms. It is practised as wet hijama (with superficial incisions) and dry cupping (suction only). Both methods are supported by extensive clinical research and recommended in Islamic Prophetic medicine.

Which hijama cup size should I use for different body areas?

Large cups (Size 3–4): Back, thighs, glutes, and chest. Medium cups (Size 2): Shoulders, knees, and calves. Small cups (Size 0–1): Face, hands, feet, and joints. Curved cups: Shoulders, knees, elbows and any uneven body surface. Our Premium Silicone Cup Set includes all four sizes in one box, making it the perfect complete solution.

How often should I do hijama therapy?

For general wellness and prevention: wet hijama once every 4–6 weeks on Sunnah days. For active conditions (pain, chronic disease): every 2–3 weeks initially, then monthly maintenance. Dry cupping can be done more frequently — 1–3 times per week for sports recovery and relaxation. Always allow at least 3–4 days between wet hijama sessions on the same body area.

What diet should I follow after hijama?

After hijama, eat light and easily digestible foods: dates, honey, black seed (Nigella sativa), warm soups, lentils, and fresh fruits. Drink plenty of warm water. Avoid red meat, heavy fried foods, dairy, cold drinks, and sugar for at least 24 hours post-session. Rest for the remainder of the day. Avoid bathing for 2–3 hours after wet hijama. Black seed oil is particularly beneficial post-hijama for its anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
