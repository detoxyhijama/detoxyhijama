Using Fire Cupping Glass Set of 16 in Your Practice

Fire Cupping Glass Set of 16 is a professional-grade hijama product trusted by thousands of practitioners across India. Primary applications: Fire cupping glass sets are used in the classical fire cupping method: a cotton ball soaked in ethanol is ignited, briefly introduced into the cup, withdrawn, and the cup is immediately placed on the patient's skin. The cooling air inside the cup contracts and creates a powerful, deep suction. This method produces stronger, more intense suction than pump methods at equivalent cup sizes — preferred by practitioners trained in traditional methods and patients who prefer the deep, warming sensation of fire cupping.

Care, Sterilisation and Storage

Borosilicate glass is virtually indestructible under normal clinical use — it will not crack from thermal cycling in fire cupping. However, glass cups will break if dropped on hard floors. Store in the padded carry bag provided and handle with care. Clean after every session by washing in warm soapy water, rinse thoroughly, then soak in hospital-grade disinfectant for 30 minutes. Inspect every cup before use for cracks, chips or rough areas on the rim — discard any damaged cup immediately.

Proper product care not only protects your patients — it significantly extends the working life of your equipment. Our manufacturing standards are designed for durability in clinical conditions, but longevity requires proper sterilisation protocols, appropriate storage, and regular inspection for wear.

We recommend maintaining a logbook for your reusable equipment, recording sterilisation dates and any observations about product condition. This practice supports infection control accountability and helps you identify when products need replacement before they become a problem in a session.

Clinical Considerations and Best Practice

Fire cupping requires specific training and should only be performed by qualified practitioners. The ethanol-soaked cotton ball must be the correct size — large enough to create adequate thermal effect but not so large as to allow burning alcohol to drip into the cup. The flame must be kept moving while inside the cup — never held stationary. The cup must be placed immediately after flame withdrawal. Keep a clean, damp cloth close to hand during fire cupping sessions. Never fire cup in the presence of supplemental oxygen or near aerosol disinfectants.

Every patient is different — their skin sensitivity, underlying conditions, pain tolerance and therapeutic goals all affect how you should use this product. Experienced practitioners develop an intuitive sense for adapting their technique to each patient; new practitioners should start conservatively and increase suction depth, session length or treatment frequency as they observe patient response.

Always conduct a full patient history and assessment before using any hijama product. Document your treatment protocol, the products used, suction levels applied, session duration and patient response for every session. This documentation protects both patient and practitioner and provides the longitudinal data needed to track therapeutic progress over multiple sessions.
